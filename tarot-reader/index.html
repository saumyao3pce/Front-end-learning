Tarot Reading


Tarot card reading is a form of cartomancy whereby practitioners use tarot cards to purportedly gain insight into the past, present or future. They formulate a question, then draw cards to interpret them for this end. A traditional tarot deck consists of 78 cards, which can be split into two groups, the Major Arcana and Minor Arcana. French-suited playing cards can also be used; as canany card system with suits assigned to identifiable elements (e.g., air, earth, fire, water).


How to Do a Tarot Reading in 7 Easy Steps


* Determine the Focus.: Ask the person you’re reading for what area of life they’d like to focus on so you have a starting point. Here’s more advice on establishing a focus.

* Shuffle Your Cards.: Shuffle your cards intuitively and in whatever way feels most comfortable. See why it’s important to shuffle your cards.

* Ask Relevant & Open-Ended Questions.: Ask the cards relevant questions and avoid yes or no questions. Discover essential advice on formulating good questions.

* Draw the Cards.: Pull cards from your deck, arrange your spread, or allow them to fall out as you shuffle. Check out the benefits of working with spreads.

* Interpret the Message.: Begin to intuitively interpret the cards and add additional depth by analyzing card symbology. Here are some pointers to help your interpretations.

* Follow Up With Advice.: Make sure you draw advice from the cards you pull and leave the person you’re reading for with a main takeaway. Find out how to identify advice from the cards.

* Express Gratitude & Close the Session.: Always end your session with gratitude for your received messages and place your deck face down. Click here for tips on closing out your session.

A few of our suggestions for tarot books?


1. "Modern Tarot" by Michelle Tea, which blends memoir and personal experience for each card.
2. "Tarot for Change" by Jessica Dore, pairing insights from psychology with tarot cards.
3. "Seventy Eight Degrees of Wisdom" by Rachel Pollack, considered by many to be the ultimate tarot authority.
4. "The Easiest Way to Learn the Tarot Ever!!" by Dusty White, an interactive and frankly hilarious workbook that makes tarot accessible
5. "Tarot Masterclass" by Paul Fenton Smith, which offers detailed breakdowns of each card's meaning as well as tips for becoming a professional tarot reader.

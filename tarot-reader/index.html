Tarot Reading


Tarot card reading is a form of cartomancy whereby practitioners use tarot cards to purportedly gain insight into the past, present or future. They formulate a question, then draw cards to interpret them for this end. A traditional tarot deck consists of 78 cards, which can be split into two groups, the Major Arcana and Minor Arcana. French-suited playing cards can also be used; as canany card system with suits assigned to identifiable elements (e.g., air, earth, fire, water).


Tarot reading is a practice that involves using a deck of cards known as tarot cards to gain insight, guidance, and understanding about various aspects of life. It is often used as a tool for self-reflection, personal growth, and decision-making. Each tarot card carries symbolic imagery and represents different archetypes, situations, or energies.



Ethical Guidelines and Considerations


Tarot reading practitioners often follow certain ethical guidelines to ensure responsible and respectful practice. Here are some considerations:

Confidentiality: Tarot readers should maintain client confidentiality and respect their privacy.

Consent: Obtain informed consent from clients before conducting a reading and make sure they understand the nature and purpose of the session.

Professionalism: Practitioners should maintain a professional demeanor, providing objective and unbiased interpretations.

Non-judgment: Tarot readers should approach clients without judgment, creating a safe space for exploration and discussion.

Empowerment: Tarot readers should strive to empower clients, encouraging them to take responsibility for their own decisions and actions.

Boundaries: Set clear boundaries regarding the scope and limitations of the reading, ensuring that clients understand the reading's purpose and its limitations in predicting the future.


How perform Tarot Reading in 7 Steps

Tarot reading works by tapping into the subconscious mind and the collective unconscious to provide guidance and insights. The reader shuffles the cards while focusing on a specific question or issue. The cards are then laid out in a specific pattern or spread, and their meanings are interpreted in relation to the question or situation at hand. The reader combines intuition, knowledge of the card meanings, and the client's input to provide a meaningful interpretation.



* Determine the Focus.: Ask the person you’re reading for what area of life they’d like to focus on so you have a starting point.

* Shuffle Your Cards.: Shuffle your cards intuitively and in whatever way feels most comfortable.

* Ask Relevant & Open-Ended Questions.: Ask the cards relevant questions and avoid yes or no questions.

* Draw the Cards.: Pull cards from your deck, arrange your spread, or allow them to fall out as you shuffle.

* Interpret the Message.: Begin to intuitively interpret the cards and add additional depth by analyzing card symbology.

* Follow Up With Advice.: Make sure you draw advice from the cards you pull and leave the person you’re reading for with a main takeaway.

* Express Gratitude & Close the Session.: Always end your session with gratitude for your received messages and place your deck face down.
